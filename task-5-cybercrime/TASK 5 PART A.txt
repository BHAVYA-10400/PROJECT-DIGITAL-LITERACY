Case Study: UPI "Request Money" Fraud

Q1. What the Crime Is?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves deceiving a victim into authorizing a transaction that drains their bank account. Unlike hacking, this crime relies on "social engineering," where the attacker manipulates the victim into voluntarily entering their UPI PIN under a false pretext.

Q2. How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)? 

1. The Bait: The fraudster finds a target on a secondary marketplace (like OLX) or social media. They pose as a buyer interested in an item the victim is selling.


2. The Deception: The attacker claims they want to send an advance payment but "accidentally" sends a "Request Money" notification to the victim's UPI app instead of a "Send Money" transaction.


3. The Manipulation: Over a phone call, the attacker confuses the victim, telling them that to receive the money, they must click the notification and enter their UPI PIN.

4. The Theft: As soon as the victim enters the PIN, they are actually authorizing a debit. The money is instantly transferred from the victim's account to the fraudster’s account.

Q3. Who is Usually Targeted ?

Targets often include college students, small-time online sellers, and elderly individuals who are familiar with using digital apps but may not understand that a PIN is only required for sending money, never for receiving it.

The Consequences

The primary consequence is immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to recover due to the instantaneous nature of UPI. Victims also experience significant psychological stress, a loss of trust in digital banking, and the risk of their personal data being sold for further identity theft
