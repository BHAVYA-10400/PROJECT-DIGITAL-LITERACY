TASK 5 PART B

Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

1.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your VIT student email, Gmail, and all social media accounts to provide an extra layer of security beyond just a password.

2.Verify UPI 'Receive' Requests: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a "buyer" on a marketplace asks you to enter a PIN to get a payment, it is a scam.

3.Secure Your UPI App: Set a strong biometric or pattern lock specifically for your UPI and banking apps to prevent unauthorized access if your phone is lost or stolen in the hostel or on campus.

4.Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank accounts or enter credit card details while connected to public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks in cafes or public transport.

5.Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create complex passwords using a mix of upper/lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password for your university portal and your personal accounts.

6.Check Link Authenticity: Before clicking on a link in an SMS or WhatsApp message claiming to be from a bank or a "job offer," verify the URL. Scammers often use "bit.ly" or misspelled domain names.

7.Keep Your OS and Apps Updated: Regularly update your Android/iOS and your laptop’s operating system to ensure you have the latest security patches against malware and ransomware.

8.Audit Your Privacy Settings: Periodically check your Instagram and LinkedIn privacy settings to ensure your phone number and location are not visible to the general public.
