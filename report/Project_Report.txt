For the given COMMUNICATION SCENARIO: A common problem with poor digital communication occurs when students use informal language or 'text-speak' in professional emails to faculty. In a hypothetical scenario, a student sent a rushed, lowercase email to a professor asking for a grade change without a subject line or proper sign-off. The professor, unable to identify the student or the course, ignored the email, causing the student to miss the formal appeal deadline. This situation could have been handled better by following standard email etiquette. The student should have included a clear subject line with their name and course code, used a formal greeting, and clearly explained their request with supporting evidence. A professional tone ensures the recipient takes the request seriously and helps avoid delays in academic or professional progress.

Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

Part A: Case Study – UPI "Request Money" Fraud For this task, I conducted a detailed case study on UPI and Online Payment Fraud, a crime that increasingly targets university students in India. This fraud involves "social engineering," where an attacker poses as a buyer on a digital marketplace like OLX. Instead of sending an advance payment, the fraudster sends a "Request Money" notification to the victim. Over a phone call, they manipulate the victim into entering their UPI PIN under the false pretext that it is required to "receive" the funds. The consequences include immediate financial loss and a serious breach of trust in digital banking systems. This case study shows that cybercrime is often more about exploiting a lack of digital literacy than about technical hacking.

Part B: Prevention and Reflection As a Student Digital Ambassador, I developed a "Stay Safe Online" Checklist featuring 8 actionable tips. This includes key measures like enabling Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all university and personal accounts, setting biometric locks on banking apps, and verifying all UPI notifications. I also included the official Indian government reporting channels: the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and the Helpline 1930.
